# Titanic Passenger Survival Dashboard

## A Story Hidden in Data

When I first looked at the Titanic dataset, I saw more than just numbers. I saw stories of 418 real people, families making life changing decisions, and survival patterns that still teach us about human behavior today.

This dashboard transforms cold statistics into visual narratives that anyone can understand.

## What This Dashboard Reveals

Imagine being able to answer these questions at a glance:

**The Survival Reality**: Only 36.4% of passengers survived. That's 152 souls who made it through that tragic night, while 266 didn't. These aren't just statistics, they're human stories.

**Gender Mattered**: The numbers tell a powerful story about "women and children first." The survival rates by gender show a stark difference that speaks to the values and chaos of that moment.

**Class Divided Fate**: First class passengers had dramatically different survival odds than third class. This dashboard makes that inequality impossible to ignore. Looking at the survival by class chart, you can see how your ticket price quite literally determined your chances.

**Age Was a Factor**: Children had different survival rates than adults. The age group analysis shows which demographics were prioritized when lifeboats were limited.

**The Economics of Survival**: With an average fare of 35.63, you can explore how much people paid and how that correlated with their survival. The fare distribution tells its own economic story.

## Why This Dashboard Works

I built this with one goal: making data accessible. No PhD required, no complex jargon. Just clean, intuitive visualizations that let the data speak.

### Key Features:

**Interactive Filters**: Want to see survival rates for just females? Or analyze first class passengers? Click and explore. The dashboard responds instantly to your curiosity.

**Visual Hierarchy**: Every chart serves a purpose. From the high level metrics at the top to the detailed breakdowns below, there's a logical flow that guides your understanding.

**Real Insights**: This isn't just pretty graphs. It's about discovering patterns that matter, understanding human behavior under pressure, and seeing how social structures affected survival.

**Clean Design**: No clutter, no confusion. Just information designed to inform.

## The Technical Side

Built entirely in Excel, this dashboard proves you don't need expensive tools to create powerful analytics. Using pivot tables, conditional formatting, and thoughtful design principles, I transformed raw data into something meaningful.

Every element was chosen deliberately:
- Color psychology to guide attention
- Chart types that match the data story
- Filters that empower exploration
- Layout that flows naturally

## What I Learned

I learned to:
